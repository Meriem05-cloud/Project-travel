El Jem Amphitheatre

View on Google Maps UNESCO Heritage

Built around 238 AD, the Amphitheatre of El Jem is the third-largest Roman amphitheater in the world — surpassed only by Rome's Colosseum and the ruined theater of Capua. This UNESCO World Heritage site stands 36 meters tall and could accommodate up to 35,000 spectators for gladiator battles and chariot races. It remains the best-preserved Roman amphitheater in Africa.

Entry: 12 TND Open: 8am–5pm (winter) 8am–7pm (summer)
Visit: 1–2 hours Easy access Summer music festival
  • Activities: Climb to the top (3 levels), explore underground chambers where gladiators and animals were kept, panoramic views of the countryside
  • Tip: Visit early morning (opens at 8am) to avoid heat and crowds. The underground chambers are cooler and fascinating!
Pro tip: Visit during the annual Symphonic Music Festival (held inside the amphitheater since 1985) — an unforgettable acoustic experience!

Mahdia Medina & Skifa Kahla

Mahdia is a coastal gem founded in the 10th century as the first capital of the Fatimid Caliphate. Perched on a narrow peninsula jutting into the Mediterranean, this historic town offers a charming blue-and-white medina, pristine beaches, and an authentic fishing port. The iconic Skifa Kahla (Dark Gateway) is a massive 44m fortified entrance to the old city.

Restaurant L'Espadon

Located in the heart of Mahdia's picturesque fishing port, L'Espadon is a beloved family-run seafood restaurant known for its ultra-fresh catch of the day. With tables overlooking the blue-and-yellow fishing boats, this authentic spot serves traditional Tunisian seafood couscous, grilled sea bream, and their signature octopus tagine.
