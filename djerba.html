El Ghriba Synagogue

View on Google Maps Ancient Heritage

El Ghriba Synagogue is the oldest synagogue in Africa, dating back to the 6th century BC according to Jewish tradition. Located in the village of Erriadh, this stunning white-and-blue sanctuary is a major pilgrimage site for Jews from around the world. The synagogue features beautiful hand-painted tiles, a sacred Torah ark, and a serene courtyard [citation:6].

Oldest in Africa Pilgrimage site Entry: 10 TND
Visit: 1 hour Dress modestly Lag BaOmer festival (May)
  • Activities: Explore the sanctuary, admire hand-painted tiles, learn about Jewish-Tunisian heritage, visit the sacred well
  • Tip: Modest dress required (cover shoulders and knees). Photography is allowed but be respectful.
Pro tip: Visit during the annual Lag BaOmer pilgrimage (May) to experience the vibrant celebrations with thousands of Jewish pilgrims from around the world!

Djerbahood & Borj El Kebir

Djerbahood is an open-air street art museum in Erriadh village, featuring over 250 murals by international artists who transformed the village's walls into vibrant canvases [citation:1]. Nearby, Borj El Kebir is a 13th-century fortress offering panoramic views over Houmt Souk port and the Mediterranean Sea [citation:6].

Restaurant El Fondouk

Housed in a beautifully restored 17th-century caravanserai in the heart of Houmt Souk, El Fondouk is one of Djerba's most atmospheric restaurants. Serving traditional Djerbian cuisine in a candlelit courtyard, this family-run establishment is famous for its octopus couscous, grilled fresh fish, and warm Tunisian hospitality [citation:3][citation:8].
